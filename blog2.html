Lack of Diversity in the Tech Industry
September 13, 2015

What John Boitnott says in his article, "8 Initiatives That Show Tech Wants to Solve its Diversity Problem,” is true. It really is no secret that the tech industry has a diversity problem. It’s a well known fact and the awareness is spreading fast especially now in this era of libertarianism. From “Girl Develop It” to even the hashtag #ilookLikeanEngineer, the attempts to fill in the gaps are increasing. However, I realized that the main focus towards increasing diversity in the tech bubble seems to be aimed towards the gender gap. As a woman entering the web development field, I even saw myself focusing on the concerns of entering the “brogrammer’s” world.

[Arrays] vs. {Hashes}
September 13, 2015

Which do you think is harder: learning Ruby or teaching a class of kindergarteners? I think they are equally challenging, but in this blog, I'd like you to do both at once in order to understand arrays and hashes. Arrays and hashes are both indexed collections. Collection means that they can store objects such as strings and integers. Indexed means that they have "keys" to access each of their stored objects. Like a key to a classroom door, you can only access an object in an array or a hash with its indexed key! Now let's unlock the door to your classroom and prepare for your first kindergarten lesson. First thing on the list: get to know your students' names.
